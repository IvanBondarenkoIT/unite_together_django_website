Partners and collaboration

This page features organizations and companies that support us in our mission to help those in need and contribute significantly to the development of our projects.

Embassy of Ukraine in Georgia 

The Embassy promotes the development of interstate relations between Ukraine and Georgia at all levels in order to ensure harmonious development of mutual relations, as well as cooperation on issues of mutual interest. The Embassy also performs consular functions.

Federal Foreign office

With offices in Berlin and Bonn and about 230 missions abroad, the Federal Foreign Office manages Germany's global relations. It promotes extensive interactions not only in politics but also in business, culture, science, and more, reflecting Germany's deep integration into international networks.

Aktion Deutschland Hilft

In 2001, Aktion Deutschland Hilft was founded by ten renowned relief aid organizations in order to provide fast and effective help in severe catastrophes. Today, our alliance of 23 aid organizations is active in over 100 countries worldwide.

ASB Georgia

Since 1992, ASB has supported Georgia initially with humanitarian aid and later by enhancing agriculture through irrigation projects. Post-1997, its focus shifted to development, emphasizing disaster preparedness and strengthening local capacities, with funding from international and German sources.

SSK Georgia

Founded in 1992, the Samaritan Association of Georgia (SSK) re-registered in 2010 as an independent NGO focusing on refugee reintegration, disaster prevention, and child welfare. In 2011, SSK launched a coalition to support disabled children and initiated a mobile nursing service in Tbilisi, also providing first-aid training and job creation for displaced individuals.

CARE Caucasus

CARE International, a 75-year-old global confederation, fights poverty and social injustice, focusing on empowering women and girls. We collaborate with communities to address poverty's roots through innovative, locally-led solutions. Our approach emphasizes transparency, accountability, and quality.

World Vision Georgia

For over 70 years, World Vision has cared for children and the needy. From its founding in 1947, when Bob Pierce helped a Chinese girl, to rescuing Vietnamese refugees in 1979, World Vision reaches where needed most. Starting in Oregon, it’s now the largest Christian NGO globally, operating in nearly 100 countries.

Action Against Hunger

Founded on the principles of humanism, freedom, equality and solidarity, promotes human dignity and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Active in many countries and committed to promoting a society enriched by diversity, it addresses critical social issues, education and humanitarian assistance with the support of the global community.

People in Need

Founded on the principles of humanism, freedom, equality and solidarity, promotes human dignity and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Active in many countries and committed to promoting a society enriched by diversity, it addresses critical social issues, education and humanitarian assistance with the support of the global community.

ADRA

The global humanitarian arm of the Seventh-day Adventist Church, this organization serves a vast community with the world’s largest health and education network. It offers relief and development assistance in over 120 countries to all people, regardless of ethnicity, politics, gender, or religion.

Helping Hand

This project aims to activate youth in Marnueli, Borjomi, and Nukriani through volunteerism, providing trainings and small grants for projects focused on integrating people with limited opportunities. Initiatives include volunteer services for day centers and those in need, and producing a motivational video, "Become a Volunteer, You Too".
